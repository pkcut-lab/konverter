## What This Tool Does

This converter translates hectares (ha) to acres and acres back to hectares, instantly and bidirectionally. Enter any value in either field and the result updates in real time. The conversion is based on the internationally defined exact relationship between the meter and the yard, which anchors both the hectare and the acre.

## Formula / How It Works

The exact relationship is derived from the international foot (1 ft = 0.3048 m exactly):

```
1 acre  = 43,560 sq ft = 4,046.8564224 m² = 0.40468564224 ha  (exact)
1 hectare = 10,000 m²
```

Therefore:

```
acres     = hectares × 2.47105
hectares  = acres × 0.404686
```

The full precision value:

```
1 ha = 2.471053814671653 acres
1 acre = 0.40468564224 ha
```

For most real-world land measurements, rounding to 5 significant figures (2.47105 and 0.40469) introduces negligible error.

## Common Use Cases

**Real estate listings and farm sales.** In the US, rural and agricultural properties are always listed in acres — a "120-acre cattle ranch" or a "2.5-acre residential lot." International buyers, investors from Europe or Latin America, or anyone comparing against global land prices needs to convert. A 120-acre ranch is 48.56 hectares.

**Agricultural production data.** The USDA publishes crop yield data in both bushels per acre and metric tons per hectare. Commodity traders and agricultural researchers constantly convert between the two. 1 acre ≈ 0.405 ha makes a useful mental anchor.

**National parks and public lands.** The US National Park System publishes park sizes in acres, while international conservation databases (IUCN protected areas) use hectares. Yellowstone National Park, at approximately 2.2 million acres, is about 890,000 hectares.

**Zoning and urban planning.** Local zoning codes in US municipalities specify lot minimums in acres (e.g., "minimum lot size: 0.25 acres"). Planners working with international partners or federal programs that use metric specifications need routine conversion.

**Carbon credit and environmental markets.** Voluntary carbon credit programs and conservation easements increasingly report sequestration in metric tons per hectare. US landowners working with these programs need to convert their acreage to hectares for reporting and pricing purposes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many acres are in a hectare?

1 hectare = 2.47105 acres. The full precision value is 2.471053814671653 acres, but 2.47105 is accurate enough for any practical land measurement.

### How many hectares are in an acre?

1 acre = 0.404686 hectares. The exact value is 0.40468564224 ha. Most agricultural and real estate contexts use 0.4047 ha as a handy approximation.

### What does one acre look like?

One acre equals 43,560 square feet. Visually, it's roughly the size of a standard American football field from goal line to goal line but without the end zones (that's about 48,000 sq ft). It's also about 90% of the area of a FIFA-standard soccer field. A standard US city block is typically 2 to 3 acres depending on the city layout.

### What does one hectare look like?

One hectare is 10,000 square meters — a perfect square 100 meters on each side. That's about 2.47 acres. In city terms, a hectare is roughly the footprint of a large city block in a US downtown grid, or about 1.5 standard American football fields.

### How big is 40 acres in hectares?

40 acres = 16.187 hectares. Forty acres carries historical and cultural weight in American history — the phrase "forty acres and a mule" refers to post-Civil War land reform proposals. In modern terms, a 40-acre parcel is a substantial rural property: 0.0625 square miles, or a square roughly 1,320 feet (about 0.25 miles) on each side.

### How do I convert hectares to acres mentally?

Multiply by 2.5 for a quick approximation — it's only about 1.2% above the exact value (2.47105). So 10 ha ≈ 25 acres (exact: 24.71 ac). For a more accurate mental calculation, multiply by 2.47.
