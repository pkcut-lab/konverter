## What This Tool Does

The Roman Numeral Converter translates integers (1–3,999) to their Roman numeral representation and back. It uses standard subtractive notation — the system codified by the Romans and still in use today — and displays a per-symbol breakdown so the result is easy to verify and understand.

## How It Works

Roman numerals use seven symbols:

| Symbol | Value |
|---|---|
| I | 1 |
| V | 5 |
| X | 10 |
| L | 50 |
| C | 100 |
| D | 500 |
| M | 1,000 |

Numbers are built by combining symbols largest-to-smallest. When a smaller symbol precedes a larger one, it is subtracted:

| Subtractive pair | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| IV | 4 | 5 − 1 |
| IX | 9 | 10 − 1 |
| XL | 40 | 50 − 10 |
| XC | 90 | 100 − 10 |
| CD | 400 | 500 − 100 |
| CM | 900 | 1,000 − 100 |

Example: 1,994 = MCMXCIV → M (1,000) + CM (900) + XC (90) + IV (4).

## What Are Common Use Cases?

**Super Bowl numbers** — The NFL uses Roman numerals for every Super Bowl. Quickly decode or encode any edition: Super Bowl L (50), Super Bowl LV (55), Super Bowl LX (60).

**Movie sequels and franchise entries** — Hollywood has a tradition of numbering sequel films in Roman numerals: Rocky II–V, Star Wars Episode I–IX, Fast & Furious XXX (hypothetically). Verify the correct numeral before writing titles.

**Clock and watch faces** — Traditional analog clocks use Roman numerals. The 12 on a standard clock is XII; the 4 is often IIII (a stylistic exception). Understanding the notation helps when describing or designing watch faces.

**Legal documents and outlines** — US legal briefs, contracts, and academic papers use Roman numerals for major section headings (Part I, Part II) and for exhibit labels. Confirm the correct form before filing.

**Copyright years in media** — Film, TV, and software copyright notices frequently use Roman numerals. MMXXIV = 2024. Decode an older copyright date to confirm when a work was published or when a rights window expires.

**Chapter and volume numbering** — Books, encyclopedias, and academic journals use Roman numerals for front matter (pages i, ii, iii) and volume numbering. Convert for citation formatting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why did the Romans not just use Arabic numerals?**
Arabic numerals (and the zero they require) were not introduced to Europe until the 10th–13th centuries via Arabic-speaking scholars. Roman numerals predate that system by over a millennium.

**Can Roman numerals handle fractions?**
The Romans had a system of fractions based on twelfths (the uncia = 1/12), using dots. However, this system is not in modern use. This tool handles only whole numbers 1–3,999.

**What comes after MMMCMXCIX?**
In standard notation, nothing — 3,999 is the limit. Medieval scholars invented vinculum notation (a bar over a numeral multiplies it by 1,000), allowing V̄ = 5,000 and M̄ = 1,000,000, but this is not part of everyday use.
