## What This Tool Does

This converter handles metric tons (tonnes) to pounds — and the reverse. It also supports conversions from short tons (US) and long tons (UK), making it useful for any context where the type of "ton" matters: international shipping, agriculture, construction materials, or commodity trading.

## How It Works

All three ton types convert to pounds using fixed, exact ratios:

| Unit | Abbreviation | Pounds (exact) | Kilograms (exact) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Metric ton (tonne) | t | 2,204.623 lbs | 1,000 kg |
| Short ton (US ton) | tn | 2,000 lbs | 907.185 kg |
| Long ton (UK ton) | LT | 2,240 lbs | 1,016.047 kg |

The converter uses these ratios directly — no intermediate rounding. Results are displayed to 4 significant figures by default, with an option to show the full precision.

## Common Use Cases

**Shipping and freight.** A container shipment from Germany is quoted at 25 metric tons. US customs documentation uses short tons. 25 metric tons = 27.56 short tons = 55,116 lbs. Knowing the distinction prevents costly classification errors.

**Agriculture.** US grain is often traded in bushels domestically but in metric tons on export markets. Corn yield data from the USDA is sometimes expressed in short tons per acre; international comparisons use metric tons per hectare.

**Construction and materials.** Structural steel is ordered in short tons in the US. A specification calling for 150 metric tons of rebar means 330,693 lbs — knowing the conversion ensures the right quantity is ordered.

**Mining and commodities.** Gold, copper, and coal prices on international commodity markets are quoted per metric ton. US domestic coal pricing often uses short tons. A $120/short-ton domestic price equals roughly $132.28/metric-ton for international comparison.

**HVAC and mechanical.** Air conditioner capacity is sometimes expressed in "tons of cooling" — this refers to neither a metric nor a short ton of weight, but to the heat absorption of 1 short ton of ice melting over 24 hours (12,000 BTU/hr). This is a separate usage and does not apply here.

## FAQ

**Is a metric ton the same as a tonne?**
Yes. "Tonne" is the international spelling used in most countries; "metric ton" is the preferred US term for the same unit. Both equal exactly 1,000 kg.

**The US uses pounds — why would I encounter metric tons?**
Any US business engaged in import/export, international agriculture, or global commodity trading will encounter metric tons. Documentation from international suppliers, shipping manifests, and commodity exchange contracts frequently use tonnes.

**How precise is this conversion?**
The ratio 1 metric ton = 2,204.6226218 lbs is exact by definition (since both kg and lbs are defined by SI constants). Results in this tool are rounded to 4 decimal places by default.
