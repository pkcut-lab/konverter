## What This Tool Does

This calculator converts an hourly wage to annual salary — and breaks the result down into monthly, bi-weekly, weekly, and daily figures. Adjust hours per week, weeks worked per year, and paid time off to match your exact situation.

## How It Works

The core formula is straightforward:

| Variable | US Standard | Your Input |
|---|---|---|
| Hours per week | 40 | Adjustable |
| Weeks per year | 52 | Adjustable |
| Total hours/year | 2,080 | Calculated |
| Annual salary | Rate × 2,080 | Displayed |

For someone earning $25/hour on a standard US full-time schedule:

- **Annual:** $25 × 2,080 = **$52,000**
- **Monthly:** $52,000 ÷ 12 = **$4,333.33**
- **Bi-weekly:** $52,000 ÷ 26 = **$2,000**
- **Weekly:** $25 × 40 = **$1,000**

## What Are Common Use Cases?

**Evaluating a job offer.** A recruiter quotes $28/hour. You want to know if that beats your current $55,000 salary. At 2,080 hours, $28/hour = $58,240/year — $3,240 more before taxes.

**Part-time and contract work.** Freelancers and part-time employees rarely work 40 hours for 52 weeks. Set your actual hours — 30 hours/week, 48 working weeks — and get a realistic annual figure.

**Comparing benefits packages.** Two offers at the same hourly rate can differ significantly in total compensation once PTO is factored in. An employer offering 15 PTO days reduces your working hours to 1,960/year vs. 2,080 — a real difference at higher rates.

**Minimum wage benchmarks.** The federal minimum wage is $7.25/hour ($15,080/year at full-time). Many states and cities set higher floors: California ($16/hour = $33,280/year), New York City ($16.50/hour = $34,320/year), Washington ($16.28/hour = $33,862/year).

**Overtime planning.** If you regularly work 45 hours/week, your overtime pay (1.5× for 5 hours) adds roughly $1.5× rate × 5 hours × 52 weeks to your base. At $20/hour, that's an extra $7,800/year in gross overtime pay.

## Häufige Fragen?

**Is 2,080 always the right divisor?**
Only for a standard 40-hour, 52-week year with no unpaid time off. If you take 2 weeks of unpaid leave, use 50 weeks (2,000 hours). Always match the divisor to your actual schedule.

**Why does my employer's annual figure differ from mine?**
Some employers calculate based on 260 working days × 8 hours. That's also 2,080 — identical. Differences arise when leap years, holiday closures, or different hour definitions are used.

**Does this work for salaried employees converting the other way?**
Yes. Divide your annual salary by 2,080 (or your actual hours worked) to find your effective hourly rate. This is useful for comparing salaried positions against hourly contract rates.
