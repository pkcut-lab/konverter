## What This Tool Does

This converter handles milliliters ↔ US fluid ounces in both directions with full precision. It is particularly useful for recipe scaling, beverage sizing, pharmaceutical dosing, and cosmetics labeling — all contexts where the US requires dual-unit labeling (mL and fl oz) on packaging.

## How Do You Convert Milliliters to US Fluid Ounces?

**US fl oz = mL ÷ 29.5735295625**

**mL = US fl oz × 29.5735295625**

The US fluid ounce is defined as exactly 1/128 of a US liquid gallon. Since a US gallon is exactly 231 cubic inches, and one inch is exactly 2.54 cm, the mL equivalent works out to the precise value above.

### Imperial Fluid Ounce (UK)

**Imperial fl oz = mL ÷ 28.4130625**

The Imperial fluid ounce is 1/20 of a UK pint, which is 1/8 of an Imperial gallon (4.54609 L). It is slightly smaller than the US fluid ounce.

### Recipe Conversion (Metric to US)

International recipes (especially European and Australian) give liquid measurements in mL. US recipes use cups and fl oz. The critical conversions:

- 250 mL ≈ 1 cup (actually 8.45 fl oz — close enough for cooking, not baking)
- 100 mL ≈ 3.38 fl oz ≈ 6.75 tablespoons
- For baking, use exact mL measurements with a kitchen scale or measuring cup with both scales

### Medicine and Supplement Dosing

Liquid medications in the US are typically dosed in mL on the label and measured with calibrated syringes or dosing cups. The FDA requires mL-only markings on pediatric liquid medicine dosing devices since 2011 to reduce dosing errors.

Common doses:
- Children's acetaminophen: 5 mL (0.17 fl oz) per dose
- Adult liquid antacid: 15–30 mL (0.5–1.0 fl oz) per dose
- Cough syrup: 10–15 mL (0.34–0.51 fl oz) per dose

Never use household teaspoons for medication — they vary from 3 to 7 mL.

## When Does the US vs. Imperial Units Difference Matter?

A 4% difference sounds small, but in large-batch cooking or pharmaceutical contexts it compounds. If a UK recipe calls for 10 Imperial fl oz and you use US fl oz:

10 Imperial fl oz × 28.4131 mL = 284.1 mL
10 US fl oz × 29.5735 mL = 295.7 mL

Difference: 11.6 mL — about 2.5 teaspoons. For baking a single cake, negligible. For a 50-gallon batch production, it becomes a significant systematic error.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does the US use fl oz instead of mL?**

The US retained fluid ounces from the British Imperial system used at the time of American independence. While US science and medicine use mL, consumer packaging and recipes kept the customary system. Dual labeling (both units) is required on US food and beverage products by FDA regulation.

**Is an fl oz the same as a weight ounce?**

No. Fluid ounces measure volume; weight ounces measure mass. 1 fl oz of water weighs approximately 1.04 ounces (29.6 grams) — close but not the same. Denser liquids (like honey or syrup) weigh more per fluid ounce.

**How many mL are in a shot?**

A standard US shot is 1.5 fl oz = 44.4 mL. A "jigger" (common bartending tool) holds 1.5 fl oz on one side and 1.0 fl oz (29.6 mL) on the other. Some establishments define a shot as 1.0 fl oz (29.6 mL) — the definition varies.
